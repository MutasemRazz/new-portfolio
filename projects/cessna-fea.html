🛩️ FEA on Cessna 172 Wing

Course: ENME 547 – Finite Element Method (Fall 2024)
Role: Structural Analyst | Tools: SolidWorks, ANSYS, OpenFOAM

✅ Objectives

Evaluate structural performance of the Cessna 172 wing under realistic flight conditions.

  • Deformation and stress distribution using FEA
  • Vibration characteristics via modal analysis
  • Aerodynamic pressure loading using 2D CFD at cruise and climb attitudes
  • Ensure mesh accuracy through a grid convergence study
  • Recommend design improvements for fatigue resistance and vibration safety

📊 Key Results

  • Deformation:
    High-speed: 73.7 mm
    Low-speed: 2.9 mm
  • Von Mises Stress:
    Peaks near structural joints under climb and cruise loads
  • Grid Convergence Index (GCI): 0.5% – ensured <5% error
  • Natural Frequencies:
    First five: 4.52 Hz to 119.63 Hz
    Critical modes: 29.5 Hz, 45.1 Hz, 46.4 Hz, 72 Hz
  • Modal Deformation: Max: 1.53 mm (Mode 4 – localized stress regions)
  • Material: Non-linear Aluminum Alloy
    Yield: 280 MPa | Tangent Modulus: 50 MPa

📦 Deliverables

  • CAD Model of simplified Cessna 172 wing (NACA 2412), split into 5 segments
  • 2D CFD Simulation to generate pressure profiles at cruise/climb (OpenFOAM)
  • FEA Simulation in ANSYS using pressure loads from CFD
  • Non-linear material modeling and large deformation enabled
  • Mesh refinement study with tetrahedral and wedge elements
  • Modal analysis to evaluate resonance and fatigue risk

🛠️ Design Recommendations

  • Add composite reinforcements near critical stress areas
  • Avoid operating in resonance frequency ranges
  • Target fatigue hotspots for structural stiffening
