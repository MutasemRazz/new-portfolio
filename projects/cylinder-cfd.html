🌪️ CFD 2D Unsteady Flow Over a Circular Cylinder

Course: Computational Fluid Dynamics
Tools: OpenFOAM, ParaView, ANSA, MATLAB

✅ Objectives

  • Simulate 2D unsteady incompressible flow over a circular cylinder using OpenFOAM.
  • Capture the Von Kármán vortex street and study vortex shedding dynamics.
  • Conduct a grid convergence study with coarse, medium, and fine meshes.
  • Compute the Strouhal number and validate results against Roshko (1954) experimental data.
  • Visualize flow behavior using ParaView for time-averaged and instantaneous fields.

📊 Key Results

  • Re: 1920                 
  • Diameter: 3.18 mm
  • Velocity: 9.15 m/s
  • Turbulence Model: SST k-ω
  • Solver: pimpleFoam (transient)
  • Selected Mesh: Medium (GCI = 0.35%)
  • Strouhal Number Validation:
    • Coarse: 0.190 (10.07% error)
    • Medium: 0.204 (3.65% error)
    • Fine: 0.217 (2.77% error)

📦 Deliverables

  • CFD case setup in OpenFOAM (geometry, meshing, BCs, solver config).
  • Mesh created in ANSA and verified via MATLAB for grid convergence.
  • FFT analysis of lift coefficient to extract vortex shedding frequency and Strouhal number.
  • Post-processed flow visuals in ParaView:
    • Instantaneous velocity contours (vortex street)
    • Time-averaged velocity fields
    • Velocity profiles at 1D, 2D, 4D, and 8D downstream
