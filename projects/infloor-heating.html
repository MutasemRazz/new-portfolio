Design of a Radiant In-Floor Heating System

Course: Thermodynamics & Heat Transfer Design Project
Role: Thermal Systems Designer | Tools: Excel, MATLAB

Objectives

  • Design a radiant in-floor heating system for a 40×60×16 ft garage/workshop in −45°C climate.
  • Maintain indoor temperature at 20°C using a glycol-water heating system embedded in concrete.
  • Calculate thermal loads and pipe flow characteristics to deliver 27,015 W of heat efficiently.
  • Optimize pipe type, length, loop design, insulation layers, and flow rates.

Key Results

  • Total Heat Supplied: 27.0 kW (matched empirical estimation of 10 W/ft²).
  • Pipe Network: 610 m of ¾” PEX in 5 full + 1 short loop.
  • Flow Rate: 23.13 L/min of 60% glycol / 40% water.
  • Temperature Range: Inlet at 79°C, outlet at 50°C.
  • Surface Floor Temp: ~43.8°C worst case, ~34°C average.
  • Pump Power: 15.3 W to overcome 2.43 m head loss.
  • Estimated Material Cost: ~$13,700 CAD.

Deliverables

  • Complete engineering analysis of thermal losses and system dynamics.
  • Thermal resistance modeling from fluid through floor to indoor air.
  • Pipe network optimization for uniform heating and pressure drop management.
  • Material and cost estimation breakdown (concrete, insulation, piping).
  • Design tables for practical sizing of future systems and selection of pump/control hardware.
